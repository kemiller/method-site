## Outline

### What executive dysfunction actually means for cooking
- Not laziness — it's a real cognitive bottleneck
- Decision fatigue before you even start (what to make, do I have ingredients, which recipe)
- Working memory overload during cooking (tracking multiple things simultaneously)
- Task initiation: the hardest part is starting

### Strategies that actually help (not just "try harder")

#### Reduce decisions before you cook
- Meal planning (even loose "pick from 3 options" lists)
- Keeping a short rotation of reliable recipes
- Grocery delivery to remove the shopping barrier

#### Reduce working memory load while cooking
- Mise en place (prep everything before you start) — and why this is hard with EF issues
- Recipes that show you what you need per step, not all at once
- Checking things off as you go
- Timers so you don't have to remember

#### Make the environment work for you
- Keep your screen visible and awake
- Reduce distractions in the kitchen
- Music/podcasts as body-doubling
- Lower the stakes: frozen vegetables count, shortcuts are fine
