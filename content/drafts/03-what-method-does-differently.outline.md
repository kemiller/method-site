# What Method Does Differently

**Target keywords**: Method recipe app, structured recipe app, recipe app for ADHD, cooking workflow app
**Goal**: Product-focused post for people who already know they want something better. Conversion-oriented.

## Outline

### The standard recipe experience (and why it fails)
- Wall of text with ingredient list separated from instructions
- You're expected to hold the whole recipe in your head
- No state — if you walk away, you have no idea where you were
- Designed for reading, not for doing

### Method's core idea: recipes as workflows
- Import any recipe (URL, photo, or manual entry) — AI structures it automatically
- "Method mode" breaks the recipe into discrete steps
- Each step shows exactly which ingredients you need, in the right amounts
- You check off steps as you go — your progress is saved

### The details that matter
- **Inline ingredients**: No scrolling back and forth. Each step tells you what you need.
- **Smart scaling**: Change the serving size, everything recalculates (including unit conversions)
- **Automatic timers**: If a step says "simmer for 20 minutes," the timer is right there
- **Screen stays on**: No tapping your phone with messy hands
- **Works offline**: Recipes sync to your device
- **AI chef**: Ask questions about the recipe mid-cook ("can I substitute X?")

### What it's not
- Not a meal planner (yet)
- Not a social network for recipes
- Not trying to be everything — it does one thing well: make the act of cooking less overwhelming
